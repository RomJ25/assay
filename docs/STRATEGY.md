# Investment Strategy

**Buy cheap + quality stocks. Hold them. Sell only when fundamentals break.**

## 1. The Complete Strategy

```
    ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                                               │
    │   BUY     when a stock enters CONVICTION BUY                  │
    │                                                               │
    │   HOLD    as long as it stays in:                              │
    │             • CONVICTION BUY                                  │
    │             • WATCH LIST                                      │
    │             • QUALITY GROWTH PREMIUM                          │
    │                                                               │
    │   SELL    only when it drops to:                              │
    │             • HOLD                                            │
    │             • VALUE TRAP                                      │
    │             • AVOID                                           │
    │                                                               │
    │   ADD     new CONVICTION BUY entries each quarter             │
    │           (the portfolio grows over time)                     │
    │                                                               │
    └───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

The portfolio accumulates winners. Stocks that appreciate and leave CB (because their value score dropped as the price rose) stay in the portfolio — they moved to WATCH LIST or QUALITY GROWTH PREMIUM, which are HOLD classifications. You only exit when the business itself deteriorates.

---

## 2. When to Buy

A stock enters the portfolio when it is classified as **CONVICTION BUY**:

- **Value Score ≥ 70** — ranked in the cheapest 30% by Earnings Yield (EBIT/EV)
- **Quality Score ≥ 70** — ranked in the top 30% by Piotroski F-Score + Gross Profitability
- **F-Score ≥ 6/9** — passes the financial health gate
- **Momentum > 25th percentile** — not in freefall

All four conditions must be met simultaneously. The screener typically finds 15-25 stocks per quarter that qualify, occasionally zero when nothing meets the bar.

**Position sizing:** Equal-weight across all positions. No concentration bets.

## 3. When to Hold

A stock stays in the portfolio as long as its classification remains in the **top three buckets**:

| Classification | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **CONVICTION BUY** | Cheap + high quality + passed gates | Hold |
| **WATCH LIST** | Cheap + moderate quality, OR downgraded by a gate | Hold |
| **QUALITY GROWTH PREMIUM** | High quality but no longer cheap (price went up) | Hold |

The most common reason a stock leaves CB is that **its price appreciated**. The value score dropped because Earnings Yield fell as the stock got more expensive. This is a GOOD thing — it means the investment is working. Selling it would mean selling your winner.

From the empirical investigation (10 quarters of data):

```
    ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │  Where dropped CB stocks went    N     Return next quarter   │
    │  ─────────────────────────────  ───    ────────────────────   │
    │  QUALITY GROWTH PREMIUM          19         +9.8%  ← hold    │
    │  WATCH LIST                      27         +5.7%  ← hold    │
    │  HOLD                             3        -25.0%  ← sell    │
    │  AVOID                            1         -3.8%  ← sell    │
    │  VALUE TRAP                       0            —   (no data) │
    └───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
    Hold signals (QGP, WL): n=46, well-supported.
    Sell signals (HOLD, AVOID, VT): n=4, directional only.
```

Stocks that moved to QGP (price went up, quality stayed high) returned **+9.8%** the next quarter. Selling them would have been the single most expensive mistake.

## 4. When to Sell

Sell **only** when the classification drops to one of these three buckets:

| Classification | Meaning | Why Sell |
|---|---|---|
| **HOLD** | Both value and quality are mid-range | No conviction in either direction |
| **VALUE TRAP** | Cheap but low quality (Q < 40) | Fundamentals are genuinely weak |
| **AVOID** | Neither cheap nor quality | Nothing to justify holding |

These represent real fundamental deterioration — the business weakened, not just the stock price moved. In our data, the 4 stocks that dropped to HOLD or AVOID averaged **-25.0%** the next quarter.

**Sample size caveat:** The sell signal evidence is thin — n=3 for HOLD, n=1 for AVOID, n=0 for VALUE TRAP. The -25% figure is directional, not statistically significant. The sell rule is based primarily on LOGIC (no fundamental reason to hold a stock that's neither cheap nor quality) supported by limited empirical data. As more quarters accumulate, this evidence will strengthen or need revision.

**Do NOT sell because:**
- The stock left CONVICTION BUY (it probably appreciated)
- The value score dropped below 70 (price went up = working as intended)
- A gate fired temporarily (F-Score can fluctuate quarter to quarter)
- The stock is "up a lot" (selling winners is the most expensive behavioral error)

## 5. Why This Works

The strategy is built on three well-documented principles:

**1. The Disposition Effect Costs 3-5%/yr**

Shefrin & Statman (1985) and Odean (1998) documented that investors systematically sell winners too early and hold losers too long. This behavioral bias costs the average investor 3.2-5.7% annually. The selective sell strategy explicitly avoids this — winners stay in the portfolio.

**2. Value Alpha Peaks in Year 1-2**

Research on alpha decay (Di Mascio & Lines) shows that value stocks generate their strongest returns through multiple expansion in the first 12-18 months after purchase. Selling a stock that moves from CB to QGP (because the multiple expanded) means cutting the investment at exactly the moment the value premium is being earned.

**3. Sell Discipline Matters More Than Sell Method**

Academic research comparing sell strategies found that fundamental stop-losses (sell when financial health declines) and price stop-losses (sell on 20% drawdown) both add approximately +12% value. Having NO sell discipline underperforms both. The key insight: any consistent, pre-committed sell rule beats ad hoc selling. Our rule — sell on HOLD/VT/AVOID — is simple, fundamental-based, and pre-committed.

## 6. The Evidence

### Strategy Comparison (11 quarters, 2023-2026)

```
    ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │  Strategy                    $100 →    CAGR     vs SPY       │
    │  ────────                    ──────    ────     ──────       │
    │  Selective sell (this doc)     $168   +20.7%    +4.3%/yr     │
    │  Quarterly rebalance          $153   +16.7%    +0.3%/yr     │
    │  Annual rebalance             $140   +12.9%    -3.5%/yr     │
    │  SPY (benchmark)              $152   +16.4%       —         │
    └───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

The selective sell strategy beat quarterly rebalancing by **+4.0%/yr** and SPY by **+4.3%/yr** over 11 quarters. The advantage comes entirely from not selling winners.

**Caveat:** 11 quarters is below the 30-quarter minimum for statistical significance. The directional evidence is strong (holding appreciated stocks > selling them), but the magnitude (+4.0%/yr) may not persist exactly. The LOGIC is robust regardless of sample size — selling stocks that went up is selling winners by definition.

### 62 Years of Factor Evidence (1963-2025)

The Fama-French value+quality factor premium over 62 years:

- **CAGR with V+Q tilt:** +17.9% vs market +10.9% (premium: **+7.1%/yr**)
- **$100 became:** $2.8M (V+Q) vs $60K (market)
- **Positive in:** 6 of 7 decades, 100% of 20-year rolling periods

The academic premium is earned through annual rebalancing — not quarterly, not monthly. The selective sell strategy aligns with this by reducing unnecessary turnover.

## 7. What Not to Do

**Do not sell because a stock appreciated.** This is the most common and most expensive mistake. If TPR (Tapestry) enters CB at V=92, Q=93 and the stock gains 30%, the value score will drop (it's no longer as cheap). TPR may move to QUALITY GROWTH PREMIUM. This is the strategy WORKING. Hold it.

**Do not sell because of a bad quarter.** The value+quality premium is negative in 37% of individual years. Eight of the last twelve years had negative annual premiums. The positive years are large enough to overcome the negative ones — but only if you hold through them.

**Do not rebalance monthly.** AQR and Vanguard research shows monthly rebalancing adds negligible value over annual, while increasing transaction costs. Quarterly monitoring with selective action is the sweet spot.

**Do not set price targets.** The strategy is fundamental-based. A stock is held because its fundamentals are strong, not because of a price prediction. Sell when fundamentals break, not when a price target is hit.

**Do not chase what's working.** When growth/momentum stocks are outperforming (as they did through 2015-2020), the temptation to abandon value+quality is intense. The disposition effect research shows this impulse is costly. The 2021-2022 value surge rewarded those who held.

## 8. Expected Experience

This is what living with the strategy actually feels like:

**Year 1:** You buy 15-20 stocks. Some immediately work, some don't. The portfolio feels unremarkable.

**Year 2:** Some stocks leave CB because they appreciated (they're now in QGP or WL). You hold them. New CB entries are added. The portfolio grows to 25-35 positions.

**Year 3:** The portfolio has 30-45 positions. Some are original purchases that have appreciated 40-80%. Some newer entries are flat or down. You've sold 3-5 stocks that dropped to HOLD/VT/AVOID. The headlines say growth stocks are outperforming and your value picks look boring.

**Year 5:** The portfolio has stabilized at 40-60 positions. The winners from years 1-2 are your largest gains. The losers you cut limited the damage. You're likely ahead of SPY, but it wasn't dramatic — the edge accumulated quietly. The portfolio feels diversified and steady.

**The pattern:** The edge does not arrive in a straight line. It arrives in bursts (2016, 2021-2022) separated by periods of underperformance (2015, 2018-2020). The selective sell discipline — holding winners, cutting losers — is what allows you to be positioned when the bursts arrive.
